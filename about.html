About

I was born and raised in Saigon, now known as Ho Chi Minh City. When I turned 14, my family and I moved across the globe to St. Petersburg, Florida, where I spent the next decade immersed in a new language and culture. I eventually returned to the bustling streets of Saigon, the place I feel most at home.

For the past four and a half years, I've led product design at Anduin, a startup bwhere we create products for fund managers, law firms, and investors in the venture capital and private equity space.

My approach to solving problems is rooted in first principles, systems thinking, and bias for action — aiming to strike the right balance between user needs and business goals. I love finding simplicity within complexity and crafting nuanced design solutions. Working with talented people on interesting and challenging problems in this niche has been immensely rewarding.

In my free time, I enjoy reading books, blogs, and catching up with my friends. I also journal to clear my thoughts, document interesting things I learn, and tend to this growing, humble digital garden. You can read about what I've been up to, view links I bookmarked, or check out tools I use.

I'm open to chats and coffee if you're in town ☺

Past life

After graduating with a Bachelor's in Biology and realizing that I didn't want to go to pharmacy school, I somehow got my first full-time job right after college at a mom-and-pop print shop thanks to the fact that I had taught myself Photoshop and Illustrator, and designed band posters for my grandpa back in school, giving me some design experience.

Photography was a big part of my life, sadly I don't have time for it these days. My photos are on Flickr (although I’ve since lost access to my account) and Instagram. Making vlogs was another hobby that gave me a lot of joy.
